What They Mean & How We Fix Them

Experiencing AdBlue warning lights or countdowns? Our guide to the most common AdBlue fault codes helps you understand what's triggering your vehicle's alerts and how our expert engineers can fix them.

P20E8 Reductant Pressure Too Low

Pressure in the AdBlue dosing system has dropped below target levels.

P207F Reductant Quality/Performance Issue

The system detects incorrect AdBlue quality or dosing performance.

P204F Reductant System Performance

Overall SCR dosing performance is outside expected parameters.

P20EE SCR NOx Catalyst Efficiency Below Threshold

NOx reduction efficiency has fallen below the accepted threshold.

P229F NOx Sensor Signal Stuck

NOx sensor readings are fixed and not responding to changes.

P20BD Reductant Heater Control Circuit A

Fault in heater control circuit A for the AdBlue system.

P20BA Reductant Heater Control Circuit B

Fault in heater control circuit B for the AdBlue system.

P2047 Reductant Injector Circuit Low

Injector circuit voltage or current is below expected values.

P204C Reductant Tank Sensor Circuit Low

Tank sensor circuit shows a low signal or open circuit.

P21AB Reductant Level Sensor Intermittent

Intermittent signal from the AdBlue level sensor.

P20B9 Reductant Heater A Control Performance

Heater A is not meeting commanded performance levels.

P20BE Reductant Heater B Control Performance

Heater B is not meeting commanded performance levels.

P205B Reductant Tank Temperature Sensor Range/Performance

Tank temperature sensor readings are out of range.

P205C Reductant Tank Temperature Sensor Circuit Low

Tank temperature sensor circuit shows low voltage.

P205D Reductant Tank Temperature Sensor Circuit High

Tank temperature sensor circuit shows high voltage.

P2200 NOx Sensor Circuit Malfunction (Bank 1)

Fault detected in NOx sensor circuit on bank 1.

P2201 NOx Sensor Range/Performance (Bank 1)

NOx sensor on bank 1 is outside expected range.
